2018-2019

The first time I ever picked up a tennis racket, I was a freshman in highschool! I ended up trying out for the high school team because one of my best friends convinced me to play!

2019-2020

In the 2019-2020 season, I started training and taking tennis a little more seriously. However, the boys' season for tennis is in the spring, and that is right when COVID started. On top of this, my mom had just started chemotherapy, which meant my family and I had to go into quarantine. I ended up not picking up a racket again until the following tennis season due to being in quarantine.

2020-2021

After not having picked up a racket for a year and hitting a massive growth spurt, I somehow ended up making the Varsity team at my high school, but didn't get much play time due to my lack of experience. This is also when I started to take the sport much more seriously and began private lessons and group training sessions outside of high school! This year, my high school team was rated as the 5th in the state for 1A, but we ended up getting beaten by the 4th-rated team in the section finals and didn't get to go to state.

2021-2022

After training a lot in the off-season, I ended up making varsity again and ended up being the starter for the 1 doubles team at my school, and my partner ended up being my best friend, who got me into playing the sport! Again, our team lost the section finals to go to state. However, my doubles partner and I ended up taking 3rd in the individual section tournament, which allowed me to end my high school career with a win!

2022-2025

After playing high school tennis, I knew I wanted to keep playing but since I only started varsity for 1 year, I didn't get many offers. I also wanted to go to a good academic school, so I chose to go to St. Thomas since I had an academic scholarship. Once I started school at St. Thomas, I found out about the club team here at the school, which is the school's top men's program since they cut the D1 team. So I ended up trying out and made the team, and ever since I've been really happy with my decision and have made some of my closest friends through the team here!

2025-2026

Finally, after working hard for 7 plus years, I am now on the A team and play 1 singles. Recently, our team qualified for the National tournament for the first time in program history! We will be flying down to Texas in April to compete in the the National Championship!
